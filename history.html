Acoustic couplers

The first modems were 'acoustic couplers' that connected to the telephone line via a landline handset. These were common as AT&T had a monopoly on phone lines, however the FCC said that electronic devices could be used on the network, as long as they used an acoustic coupler.

The Smartmodem

The next major advance in modems was the Hayes Smartmodem, introduced in 1981. The Smartmodem was an otherwise standard 103A 300-bit/s direct-connect modem, but it was attached to a small microcontroller that watched the data stream for certain character strings representing commands. This allowed both data and commands to be sent through a single serial port.
